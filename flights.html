How to Find Cheaper Flights

Real advice from experienced travelers to help you save on airfare.

1

Be Flexible with Dates

Changing your travel dates by just 1-3 days can save you hundreds of dollars. Mid-week flights (Tuesday-Thursday) are typically cheaper than weekend flights.

  • Use flexible date search in our widget
  • Avoid school holidays and peak seasons
  • Consider shoulder season travel
2

Book at the Right Time

For domestic flights: 1-3 months in advance. For international: 2-8 months in advance. Set up price alerts to track fares.

  • Best booking window: 6-8 weeks out for domestic
  • International: 3-5 months for Europe, 5-8 for Asia
  • Last-minute deals exist but are risky
3

Compare Nearby Airports

Check flights from alternative airports within driving distance. Sometimes a 1-hour drive can save you significant money.

  • Major hubs often have better pricing
  • Secondary airports may have lower fees
  • Factor in ground transportation costs
4

Understand Fare Classes

Airlines have multiple fare classes with different rules and prices. Basic economy saves money but has restrictions.

  • Basic economy: No changes, seat selection extra
  • Main cabin: Standard ticket with more flexibility
  • Premium: Better seats, more baggage, flexibility
5

Consider Connecting Flights

Non-stop flights are convenient but often more expensive. A single connection can save 20-40% on longer routes.

  • Weigh time savings vs. cost savings
  • Check connection times (minimum 60-90 minutes)
  • Consider airport transfer convenience
6

Check Baggage Policies

Low-cost carriers often charge extra for bags. Factor these costs when comparing prices.

  • Most legacy airlines include 1 checked bag
  • Budget airlines charge for everything
  • Compare total cost with bags included

Flight Booking FAQs

Common questions about flight pricing, airlines, and policies.

Why do flight prices change so frequently? ▼

Airlines use dynamic pricing algorithms that adjust fares based on demand, seat availability, booking patterns, competitor pricing, and time until departure. Prices can change multiple times per day. That's why we recommend booking when you find a good price rather than waiting.

Are budget airlines safe and reliable? ▼

Yes, budget airlines must meet the same safety standards as legacy carriers. However, they operate on a different business model with fewer included services. You'll pay extra for bags, seat selection, meals, and sometimes even carry-ons. Consider the total cost including extras when comparing prices.

What happens if my flight is cancelled? ▼

If the airline cancels your flight, you're entitled to either a full refund or rebooking on the next available flight at no extra cost. For significant delays or cancellations within the airline's control, you may also be entitled to compensation under regulations like EU 261/2004 (for European flights) or similar passenger rights laws in other regions.

Should I book directly with the airline or through an agency? ▼

Both options have pros and cons. Booking directly with the airline makes it easier to manage changes and access customer service. Online travel agencies sometimes offer better bundle deals or lower prices. We show both options so you can choose based on price and your preference for service.

What's included in the price shown? ▼

We show total prices including all mandatory taxes and fees. This includes government taxes, airport fees, and fuel surcharges. Optional extras like seat selection, baggage, meals, and travel insurance are not included unless specifically stated. Always check the airline's baggage policy before booking.

Can I get a refund if I find a cheaper price later? ▼

Most airline tickets are non-refundable unless you purchase a flexible fare. Some airlines offer 24-hour free cancellation for flights departing from or to the United States. After that period, changes or cancellations typically incur fees. We recommend using price alerts before booking and purchasing travel insurance for unexpected changes.
