How Flight Comparison Works: A Complete Guide

As someone who has booked hundreds of flights over the years, I've learned that understanding how flight search engines work is the key to finding the best deals. This guide will explain the process and share actionable strategies.

Understanding Flight Search Engines

Flight comparison sites like ours don't actually sell tickets. Instead, we search hundreds of airlines and online travel agencies (OTAs) simultaneously to show you all available options. Here's what happens behind the scenes:

  1. Query Distribution: When you search, our system sends your request to multiple Global Distribution Systems (GDS) like Amadeus, Sabre, and Travelport.
  2. Real-time Pricing: Airlines update their prices constantly based on demand, seat availability, and competition.
  3. Fare Aggregation: We also check online travel agencies who sometimes have access to different inventory or special deals.
  4. Result Compilation: All options are sorted by price, duration, or your preferred criteria.

Important Note

Prices can change rapidly. The fare you see might be different when you click through to book. This is normal - airlines adjust prices based on real-time demand. Always book when you see a good price rather than waiting.

When to Book Cheap Flights: The Data

Based on analysis of millions of flights, here are the optimal booking windows:

Domestic Flights (USA/Canada/Europe)

  • Best time: 1-3 months in advance
  • Sweet spot: 6-7 weeks before departure
  • Avoid: Last-minute bookings (unless there's a sale)

International Flights

  • Best time: 2-5 months in advance
  • Europe/Asia: 3-4 months ahead
  • Australia/NZ: 4-5 months ahead
  • Peak season: Book 5-6 months early

Day of Week Matters

Statistical analysis shows:

  • Cheapest days to book: Tuesday and Wednesday
  • Most expensive: Friday and Sunday
  • Cheapest days to fly: Tuesday, Wednesday, Saturday
  • Most expensive to fly: Friday and Sunday

Flexible Date Strategy: The Secret to Big Savings

Being flexible with dates is the single most effective way to save money. Here's how to do it:

1. Use the Calendar View

Always check prices for +/- 3 days from your preferred dates. You might save 30-50% by shifting by just one day.

2. Consider Nearby Airports

Flying into or out of alternative airports can yield significant savings:

  • New York: Compare JFK, LGA, EWR, and even PHL
  • London: Compare LHR, LGW, STN, LTN
  • California: Compare LAX, SNA, BUR, ONT

3. Shoulder Season Travel

Traveling just before or after peak season can mean:

  • 40-60% cheaper flights
  • Better weather than off-season
  • Fewer crowds than peak season

Airline Loyalty vs. Price Shopping

This is a common dilemma. My advice after years of travel:

  • For business travelers: Stick with one alliance for status benefits
  • For leisure travelers: Always price shop - loyalty rarely pays off for occasional travelers
  • Consider: The value of checked bags, seat selection, and change fees

Booking Direct vs. Through Third Parties

There are pros and cons to each:

Booking Direct

Pros:

  • Easier changes/cancellations
  • Direct customer service
  • Sometimes better seat selection

Third-Party Sites

Pros:

  • Often cheaper prices
  • Bundle deals (flight+hotel)
  • Price comparison in one place

My Recommendation

Use comparison sites like ours to find the best deal, then check the airline's website directly. Sometimes they'll match or beat the price. If there's a significant difference (more than 10%), book through the cheaper option but understand the trade-offs.

Hidden City Ticketing & Skiplagging

A word of caution: Some websites promote "hidden city" ticketing (booking a flight with a connection where you get off at the connection point). While sometimes cheaper, this practice:

  • Violates airline terms of service
  • Can result in cancelled return flights
  • May lead to loyalty program termination
  • Doesn't work with checked baggage

I don't recommend this strategy for most travelers due to the risks involved.

Final Tips for Flight Booking

  1. Clear cookies or use incognito mode - Some sites track searches and may raise prices
  2. Set price alerts - Get notified when prices drop
  3. Check multiple devices - Sometimes mobile apps have different deals
  4. Consider red-eyes and odd hours - Often 20-30% cheaper
  5. Book connecting flights separately - Sometimes cheaper but more risky
