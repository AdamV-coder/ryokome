Cookie Policy

Last Updated: January 14, 2026

What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit websites. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

Cookies serve various purposes, such as remembering your preferences, analyzing how you use the website, and enabling certain features. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you can modify your browser settings to decline cookies if you prefer.

How We Use Cookies

Ryokome uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your browsing experience, analyze website performance, and understand how visitors interact with our site. These technologies help us improve our services and provide relevant information to users.

Types of Cookies We Use

Essential Cookies

These cookies are necessary for the website to function properly. They enable core functionality such as navigation, security features, and access to certain areas of the website. The website cannot function properly without these cookies, and they are set automatically when you visit.

Examples include:

  • Session management cookies
  • Security cookies to prevent fraudulent activity
  • Load balancing cookies to ensure stable website performance

Analytics and Performance Cookies

These cookies collect information about how visitors use our website, such as which pages are visited most often, how long users stay on pages, and whether they encounter error messages. This information is aggregated and anonymous.

We use this data to:

  • Understand visitor behavior and preferences
  • Identify and fix technical issues
  • Improve website navigation and content
  • Analyze traffic patterns and trends

Affiliate and Tracking Cookies

As explained in our Affiliate Disclosure, we participate in affiliate marketing programs. Cookies are used to track when you click on affiliate links and whether you complete a booking with our partners. This allows us to receive credit for referrals and earn commissions.

These cookies:

  • Record when you click on partner links
  • Track conversions (completed bookings) for commission purposes
  • Do not contain personally identifiable information
  • Expire after a set period (typically 30-90 days)

Third-Party Cookies

When you use search widgets and tools on our website provided by affiliate partners, those third parties may set their own cookies. These cookies are governed by the privacy policies of the respective third parties.

Third-party cookies may be set by:

  • Flight search providers
  • Car rental comparison services
  • Analytics platforms
  • Advertising and marketing services

We do not control these cookies or have access to the data they collect. Please review the privacy policies of our partners to understand their cookie practices.

Cookie Duration

Session Cookies

Session cookies are temporary and are deleted from your device when you close your web browser. They are used to maintain your session as you navigate through pages on our website.

Persistent Cookies

Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period specified in the cookie or until you manually delete them. They are used to remember your preferences and recognize you when you return to our website.

Persistent cookies we use typically expire after:

  • Analytics cookies: 1-2 years
  • Affiliate tracking cookies: 30-90 days
  • Preference cookies: 1 year

Managing Cookie Preferences

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can configure your browser to:

  • Block all cookies
  • Block only third-party cookies
  • Delete cookies when you close your browser
  • Notify you before a cookie is set

Please note that blocking or deleting cookies may impact your ability to use certain features of our website and may affect your browsing experience.

Browser-Specific Instructions

To manage cookies in your specific browser, consult the help documentation for:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari (desktop and mobile)
  • Microsoft Edge
  • Opera

Instructions are typically found in the browser's settings or preferences menu under sections labeled Privacy, Security, or Cookies.

Other Tracking Technologies

Web Beacons and Pixels

In addition to cookies, we may use web beacons (also known as tracking pixels or clear GIFs). These are tiny graphics embedded in web pages or emails that track whether content has been viewed or an action has been taken.

Web beacons are used to:

  • Measure website traffic and user behavior
  • Track the effectiveness of marketing campaigns
  • Understand email engagement (if we send communications)

Local Storage

We may use local storage technologies (such as HTML5 local storage) to store information on your device. Local storage is similar to cookies but can store larger amounts of data and is not automatically transmitted to the server with every request.

Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers offer a Do Not Track (DNT) signal that sends a message to websites requesting that your browsing not be tracked. Currently, there is no industry consensus on how to respond to DNT signals, and our website does not respond to or alter its behavior based on DNT settings in your browser.

Impact of Declining Cookies

If you choose to disable cookies, you can still browse Ryokome and view content. However, certain features may not work as intended:

  • Search preferences may not be saved between sessions
  • We may not be able to track affiliate referrals accurately
  • Website performance and user experience may be impacted
  • Third-party widgets and tools may not function properly

Updates to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, technologies, or legal requirements. When we make changes, we will update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page.

We encourage you to review this Cookie Policy periodically to stay informed about how we use cookies and similar technologies.
