What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, or other device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

First-Party Cookies
Cookies set by the website you're visiting (ryokome.com). These help the website function properly and remember your preferences.
Third-Party Cookies
Cookies set by other websites and services we use, such as analytics providers and affiliate partners.
Session Cookies
Temporary cookies that are deleted when you close your browser. These help with navigation during your visit.
Persistent Cookies
Cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them. These remember your preferences across visits.

How to Manage Cookies

You have several options for managing cookies on your device. Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings preferences.

Browser-Specific Instructions

  • Google Chrome
    Click the three dots in the top right ‚Üí Settings ‚Üí Privacy and security ‚Üí Cookies and other site data. Here you can block all cookies, block third-party cookies, or clear cookies when you close Chrome.
  • Mozilla Firefox
    Click the menu button ‚Üí Settings ‚Üí Privacy & Security ‚Üí Cookies and Site Data. You can choose to block cookies, block third-party cookies, or accept cookies from visited sites only.
  • Apple Safari
    Choose Safari ‚Üí Preferences ‚Üí Privacy. You can then choose to block all cookies, block third-party cookies, or prevent tracking.
  • Microsoft Edge
    Click the three dots ‚Üí Settings ‚Üí Cookies and site permissions ‚Üí Cookies and site data. You can block all cookies, block third-party cookies, or clear cookies when you close Edge.

Important Considerations

Essential Cookies: If you choose to block essential cookies, some parts of our website may not work properly. These cookies are necessary for basic functionality like page navigation and security.

Affiliate Tracking: If you disable affiliate tracking cookies, our affiliate partners may not be able to track your referral from our site. This doesn't affect the price you pay but may affect our ability to earn commissions.

Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that lets you tell websites you do not want to have your online activities tracked. Currently, there is no standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals. Like many other websites, Ryokome does not currently respond to DNT signals.
