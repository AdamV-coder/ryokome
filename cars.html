Rental Car Pickup Checklist

Follow this checklist when picking up your rental car to avoid issues and extra charges.

1

Required Documents

Bring your driver's license, passport, credit card in the driver's name, and International Driving Permit if required. Most companies require the driver to be present with all documents.

2

Fuel Policy Verification

Confirm the fuel policy: full-to-full, full-to-empty, or pre-purchase. Full-to-full is usually best. Note the current fuel level and return requirements.

3

Damage Inspection

Walk around the car with a representative. Point out all existing damage and ensure it's documented on the rental agreement. Take your own photos as backup.

4

Mileage Limits & Extras

Check for daily or total mileage limits. Confirm any additional drivers, child seats, GPS, or other extras you've booked. Verify costs for each.

5

Emergency Information

Get the rental company's 24/7 emergency number. Locate the spare tire, jack, and emergency kit. Understand the roadside assistance process.
