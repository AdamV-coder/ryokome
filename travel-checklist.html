Complete Travel Checklist

Everything you need to prepare for safe, organized, and stress-free travel, whether domestic or international.

Thorough preparation is the foundation of successful travel. This comprehensive checklist covers essential items and tasks for both domestic and international trips. While not every item applies to every journey, reviewing each section helps ensure you have not overlooked critical preparations.

Start your preparation at least 2-4 weeks before departure for international travel, or 1-2 weeks for domestic trips. Last-minute preparation often leads to forgotten items, unnecessary stress, and potentially serious problems like expired documents or missing required vaccinations.

Travel Documents

Proper documentation is non-negotiable for travel. Missing or invalid documents can prevent you from boarding flights, entering countries, or returning home.

Essential Documents

  • Valid passport (must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your return date for most international destinations)
  • Visa or travel authorization if required for your destination (check government travel advisory websites)
  • Photo identification (driver's license or government ID for domestic travel)
  • Flight confirmations and boarding passes (printed or digital copies)
  • Hotel reservations and booking confirmations
  • Travel insurance policy documents and emergency contact numbers
  • International Driving Permit if planning to rent a car abroad
  • Vaccination certificates or health documentation if required by destination country

The U.S. Department of State and similar government agencies in other countries provide country-specific entry requirements, including visa policies, passport validity rules, and current travel advisories. Always verify requirements directly with official government sources rather than relying on third-party information, as rules change frequently.

Document Security and Backups

Keep physical copies of important documents separate from originals. Store digital copies in secure cloud storage or email them to yourself so they are accessible if originals are lost or stolen. Consider leaving copies with a trusted contact at home who can send them to you in an emergency.

Many travelers photograph their passport, credit cards, and important documents and store the images in encrypted cloud storage. This provides quick access to document numbers and information when dealing with embassies or card companies if items are lost.

Flights and Transportation

Verify all transportation arrangements well before departure to catch errors or schedule changes early enough to address them.

  • Confirm flight times and terminal information (airlines sometimes change schedules)
  • Complete online check-in 24 hours before departure when available
  • Verify baggage allowance and fees for your specific ticket type
  • Arrange airport parking or transportation to the airport
  • Book airport transfers or ground transportation at your destination
  • Reserve rental cars in advance if needed
  • Download airline apps for real-time flight updates and mobile boarding passes

Airlines regularly adjust schedules, and receiving notification of changes depends on having current contact information on file. Check your reservation directly on the airline's website a few days before departure rather than relying solely on email notifications.

For information on comparing flight options and finding competitive fares, visit our flight search page. Ground transportation options including rental cars and airport transfers are covered on our car rentals and airport transfers pages.

Accommodation Confirmation

Verify that all accommodation bookings are confirmed and paid for, or that payment arrangements are clear.

  • Confirm hotel or vacation rental reservations
  • Verify check-in and check-out times
  • Understand cancellation and refund policies
  • Note the accommodation's address and contact information
  • Save directions from the airport to your hotel
  • Check if the property requires deposits or prepayment

Some hotels require a credit card on file even for prepaid reservations to cover incidentals. Verify that the card you plan to use will not expire during your stay and that it will work internationally if traveling abroad.

Health and Medical Preparation

Health preparation is particularly important for international travel but should not be overlooked for domestic trips either.

Before You Travel

  • Schedule a check-up with your doctor, especially for extended international trips
  • Obtain required vaccinations (some require multiple doses over several weeks)
  • Get prescriptions filled and ensure you have enough medication for your entire trip plus extras
  • Request a written prescription from your doctor to carry with medications
  • Purchase travel health insurance if your regular insurance does not cover international care
  • Research hospitals and medical facilities at your destination
  • Pack a basic first-aid kit with bandages, pain relievers, antiseptic, and any personal medications

The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) provides destination-specific health recommendations, required vaccinations, and disease outbreak information. Check their travel health notices 4-6 weeks before departure to allow time for any necessary vaccinations.

For international travel, the World Health Organization offers global health guidance and vaccination requirements by country.

What to Pack in Your Medical Kit

  • Prescription medications in original containers
  • Over-the-counter pain relievers and fever reducers
  • Antihistamines for allergic reactions
  • Anti-diarrheal medication
  • Motion sickness medication if prone to travel sickness
  • Basic wound care supplies (bandages, antibiotic ointment, gauze)
  • Insect repellent containing DEET for tropical destinations
  • Sunscreen with appropriate SPF for your destination climate
  • Hand sanitizer and disinfecting wipes

Keep medications in carry-on luggage rather than checked bags to ensure access during the flight and in case checked luggage is delayed or lost.

Money and Financial Preparation

Financial preparation prevents problems with accessing funds abroad and helps you avoid unnecessary fees.

Before Departure

  • Notify your bank and credit card companies of travel dates and destinations to prevent fraud blocks
  • Verify daily withdrawal limits on debit cards
  • Understand foreign transaction fees on your cards
  • Order some local currency if traveling internationally (enough for initial expenses)
  • Locate ATMs at your destination that accept your card network
  • Set up mobile banking and card lock features
  • Store emergency contact numbers for card companies
  • Bring at least two different payment methods (cards from different issuers)

Many travelers use credit cards for most international purchases because they often offer better exchange rates than currency exchange services and include fraud protection. However, not all merchants worldwide accept cards, so always carry some local cash.

Financial Safety

Never carry all your money and cards in one place. Distribute cash, cards, and backups across different bags and pockets. Consider a money belt or hidden pouch for passport and emergency cash. Leave unnecessary credit cards, extra identification, and valuable items at home or in a hotel safe.

Electronics and Communication

Modern travel often depends on electronic devices for navigation, communication, and access to important information.

  • Smartphone with international capabilities or local SIM card options
  • Portable charger or power bank (fully charged before departure)
  • Charging cables and adapters for all devices
  • Universal power adapter for international travel (electrical outlets vary by country)
  • Laptop or tablet if needed for work
  • Headphones or earbuds for flights and entertainment
  • Camera if not using smartphone for photos
  • E-reader for long flights or downtime

Download offline maps, translation apps, and entertainment before departure. Airplane Wi-Fi is often expensive and unreliable, and you may not have data access immediately upon arrival in a foreign country.

Verify that your mobile plan covers international roaming or purchase an international plan before departure. Unexpected roaming charges can be extremely expensive. Many travelers use local SIM cards or portable Wi-Fi hotspots for affordable data abroad.

Packing Essentials

Efficient packing reduces stress and baggage fees while ensuring you have what you need.

Clothing Strategy

Check the weather forecast for your destination and pack accordingly, but remember that forecasts become less reliable beyond 7-10 days. Pack versatile items that can be mixed and matched, and include one outfit more than you think you need for unexpected delays or messy situations.

  • Weather-appropriate clothing for all planned activities
  • Comfortable walking shoes (broken in before the trip)
  • Sleepwear and undergarments (including extras)
  • Light jacket or sweater (useful even in warm climates for air-conditioned spaces)
  • Swimwear if relevant to your destination
  • Formal outfit if you plan to dine at upscale restaurants
  • Rain jacket or compact umbrella

Toiletries

Remember that liquids in carry-on bags must comply with TSA regulations: containers of 3.4 ounces (100ml) or less, all fitting in a single quart-sized clear plastic bag. Consider purchasing toiletries at your destination if traveling with only carry-on luggage.

  • Toothbrush, toothpaste, and dental floss
  • Shampoo, conditioner, and body wash (in travel sizes)
  • Deodorant
  • Razor and shaving cream if needed
  • Hair styling products and tools
  • Skincare products and cosmetics
  • Contact lenses, solution, and glasses if applicable
  • Feminine hygiene products

Other Essentials

  • Travel pillow and eye mask for flights
  • Reusable water bottle (empty through security, fill after)
  • Snacks for flights and unexpected delays
  • Plastic bags for dirty laundry or wet items
  • Small locks for luggage (TSA-approved for U.S. travel)
  • Laundry detergent packets for washing clothes
  • Sewing kit for emergency clothing repairs

Pre-Departure Tasks

Complete these tasks in the days and weeks before departure to ensure your home is secure and your absence will not cause problems.

One to Two Weeks Before

  • Arrange pet care or boarding
  • Hold mail and newspaper delivery or ask a neighbor to collect them
  • Set timers on lights to make your home appear occupied
  • Inform trusted neighbors of your absence and provide emergency contact information
  • Arrange for lawn care if traveling during growing season
  • Pay bills that will come due during your absence
  • Set up automatic payments where possible

One to Two Days Before

  • Check flight status for any schedule changes
  • Verify weather at your destination and adjust packing if needed
  • Charge all electronic devices
  • Weigh luggage to ensure it is within airline limits
  • Clean out refrigerator of perishable items
  • Take out trash to avoid returning to unpleasant odors
  • Adjust thermostat to save energy while away
  • Unplug non-essential electronics

Day of Departure

  • Double-check passport, tickets, and wallet are in carry-on bag
  • Turn off water supply if traveling for extended periods
  • Lock all windows and doors
  • Activate home security system
  • Take out remaining trash
  • Turn off lights (except those on timers)
  • Leave emergency contact information with a trusted person

Safety and Security Considerations

Personal safety should be a priority throughout your journey, starting with pre-trip research and preparation.

Research your destination's safety profile through official government travel advisory websites. The U.S. State Department, UK Foreign Office, and similar agencies provide up-to-date information on political stability, crime rates, areas to avoid, and current safety concerns. Register with your embassy or consulate if traveling to areas with elevated risk.

  • Research common scams and safety issues at your destination
  • Understand local laws and customs to avoid unintentional violations
  • Share your itinerary with family or friends
  • Know the local emergency numbers (equivalent to 911)
  • Locate your country's embassy or consulate at your destination
  • Purchase travel insurance that includes emergency evacuation
  • Keep valuables secured and be aware of pickpocket techniques common in tourist areas

Trust your instincts. If a situation feels unsafe or uncomfortable, remove yourself from it. Avoid displaying expensive jewelry, electronics, or large amounts of cash. Stay in well-lit, populated areas, especially at night, and be cautious about sharing your travel plans with strangers.

Final Preparation Notes

This checklist is intentionally comprehensive to cover various travel scenarios. Not every item will apply to your specific trip. Use it as a starting point and customize it based on your destination, trip duration, planned activities, and personal needs.

Experienced travelers often keep a master packing list that they refine over time, making preparation for each new trip faster and more efficient. Consider creating your own personalized checklist based on this foundation, adding items specific to your travel style and requirements.
