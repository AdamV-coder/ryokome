Don't leave home unprepared. Use our comprehensive travel checklist developed from years of international travel experience. Covering documents, health, flights, money, and electronics to ensure a smooth journey.

Last updated: December 2023 | Based on international travel best practices

Proper preparation is the foundation of successful international travel. This comprehensive checklist, developed from extensive travel experience across 50+ countries, covers every essential aspect of trip preparation. Whether you're a first-time international traveler or a seasoned globetrotter, following this guide will minimize stress and maximize enjoyment.

1. Essential Travel Documents

Document preparation should begin at least 6-8 weeks before departure, especially for visa applications. Missing or incorrect documents can result in denied boarding or entry.

1

Valid Passport

Ensure your passport is valid for at least 6 months beyond your return date. Many countries enforce this rule strictly. Check expiration dates for all traveling family members.

Expert Tip: Photocopy the photo page of your passport and store separately from the original. Consider using a U.S. State Department passport card as secondary ID.
2

Visas & Entry Requirements

Research destination-specific requirements. Many countries now offer e-Visas, while others require embassy applications. Processing times vary from 24 hours to several weeks.

Resource: Check the U.S. State Department country pages for official requirements.
3

International Driving Permit (IDP)

Required in over 150 countries when renting cars. Must be accompanied by your valid domestic license. Obtain from AAA or your national automobile association.

Note: IDPs are valid for one year and cannot be obtained abroad. Apply 2-3 weeks before departure.
4

Travel Insurance Documents

Carry printed copies of your policy, including coverage details, emergency contact numbers, and claim procedures. Save digital copies in cloud storage.

Essential: Verify medical evacuation coverage limits ‚Äì adequate coverage typically starts at $100,000.

2. Health & Medical Preparation

Medical emergencies abroad can be costly and complex. Proper health preparation is non-negotiable for international travel.

Critical Medical Notice

Most domestic health insurance provides minimal coverage abroad. A medical evacuation alone can cost $15,000-$200,000. Travel medical insurance is essential, not optional.

1

Vaccinations & Health Advisories

Check CDC travel health notices 6-8 weeks before travel. Some vaccinations require multiple doses over several weeks.

Required: Yellow fever vaccination certificates for certain African and South American countries.
2

Prescription Medications

Carry medications in original containers with pharmacy labels. Bring documentation from your doctor for controlled substances. Pack extra supplies (7-10 days beyond trip length).

Important: Check if your medications are legal at your destination. Some common prescriptions are banned in certain countries.
3

Medical Information & Contacts

Carry a list of medical conditions, allergies, blood type, and emergency contacts. Include your doctor's contact information and insurance policy details.

Smart Traveler: Register with the Smart Traveler Enrollment Program (STEP) for emergency alerts.

3. Flight & Transportation Preparation

Air travel requires careful planning beyond just booking tickets. These steps ensure smoother airport experiences and better flight management.

1

Flight Confirmations & Check-in

Check-in online 24 hours before departure when possible. Download boarding passes to your phone and carry printed backups. Verify terminal information day-of.

Pro Tip: Set flight status alerts through airline apps or services like FlightStats.
2

Baggage Requirements & Fees

Know your airline's baggage allowance, weight limits, and prohibited items. Measure and weigh bags before leaving home to avoid costly airport fees.

Save Money: Many airlines charge less for checked bags purchased online versus at the airport.
3

Airport Transfer Arrangements

Pre-book airport transfers, especially for late arrivals or unfamiliar destinations. Have local currency for taxis and know public transport options.

Safety First: Pre-booked transfers are often safer than hailing taxis in unfamiliar countries.

Ryokome's Airport Security Tip

Arrive at international airports 3 hours before departure. This allows time for enhanced security, document checks, and unexpected queues. Keep essential documents (passport, boarding pass, visa) in an easily accessible but secure location. Consider TSA PreCheck or Global Entry for faster security processing on future trips.

4. Money & Financial Preparation

Financial preparation involves more than just carrying cash. A layered approach ensures access to funds while protecting against loss or theft.

1

Multiple Payment Methods

Carry 2-3 credit cards from different issuers (Visa, Mastercard, American Express). Notify banks of travel plans to avoid fraud alerts blocking cards.

Smart Strategy: Use credit cards with no foreign transaction fees and travel benefits like rental car insurance.
2

Local Currency Strategy

Obtain some local currency before departure for immediate expenses. Use ATMs at major banks (not standalone machines) for better rates and security.

Best Practice: Avoid airport currency exchanges - rates are typically 5-15% worse than city centers.
3

Emergency Cash Reserve

Keep $200-$500 USD in small bills as emergency backup. Store separately from primary wallet. US dollars are widely accepted for emergencies worldwide.

Security: Consider a hidden money belt or neck wallet for emergency cash and backup credit card.

5. Electronics & Connectivity

In our connected world, electronic preparation is crucial for communication, navigation, and entertainment while traveling.

1

Power Adapters & Converters

Research outlet types and voltage requirements at your destination. Universal adapters are convenient but verify they work with your specific devices.

Critical: Most modern electronics (phones, laptops) support 100-240V - check labels. Hair dryers and curling irons often require converters.
2

Communication Solutions

Unlock your phone for international use or purchase a local SIM card. Download offline maps, translation apps, and essential travel apps before departure.

Free Option: Use WhatsApp, Signal, or Telegram for free messaging over Wi-Fi. Download Google Translate's offline language packs.
3

Data Backup & Security

Backup phone and camera data to cloud services daily. Enable device tracking (Find My iPhone, Find My Device). Use VPN on public Wi-Fi networks.

Security: Password-protect all devices and consider installing tracking apps like Prey in case of theft.

Final 24-Hour Checklist

Complete these items within 24 hours of departure for a stress-free start to your journey.

Home Preparation

Stop mail delivery, set lights on timers, arrange pet care, empty refrigerator perishables, set thermostat, and notify a trusted neighbor.

Travel Day Essentials

Pack charging cables in carry-on, fill water bottle after security, wear comfortable shoes, and carry essential medications in original containers.

Digital Preparation

Share itinerary with emergency contacts, download entertainment for flight, charge all devices fully, and enable travel notifications on credit cards.

This comprehensive travel checklist represents distilled wisdom from thousands of international journeys. While individual needs may vary, following these guidelines will significantly reduce travel stress and increase trip enjoyment. Safe travels from the Ryokome team!
