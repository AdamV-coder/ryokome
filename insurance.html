Why Travel Insurance Is Critical

Medical emergencies abroad can cost tens of thousands of dollars. Most domestic health insurance provides limited or no coverage outside your home country. Travel insurance protects you from financial ruin due to unexpected medical bills, trip cancellations, or lost belongings.

According to the U.S. Department of State, the average cost of medical evacuation is between $15,000 and $200,000 depending on location and condition. Travel insurance typically covers this for a fraction of the cost.

Who Needs Travel Insurance

Certain travelers benefit significantly from comprehensive coverage.

1

International Travelers

When traveling outside your home country, your domestic health insurance likely provides little to no coverage. Medical emergencies abroad can be financially devastating without insurance.

Example: A broken leg requiring surgery in Europe can cost $15,000-$30,000 without insurance.
2

Travelers with Pre-Existing Conditions

Many policies offer coverage for stable pre-existing conditions if purchased within a specific time frame after initial trip payment (typically 10-21 days).

Important: Declare all conditions honestly. Non-disclosure can invalidate your entire policy.
3

Adventure & Sports Enthusiasts

Standard policies may exclude high-risk activities. Adventure sports riders provide coverage for skiing, scuba diving, hiking, and other activities.

Check: Always verify if your planned activities are covered. Many require additional premiums.
4

Business Travelers & Digital Nomads

Extended trips, multiple destinations, and expensive equipment require specialized coverage. Annual multi-trip policies can be cost-effective for frequent travelers.

Option: Annual policies cover unlimited trips (with duration limits per trip) for one yearly premium.

Travel Insurance FAQs

Common questions about coverage, claims, and policies.

Does travel insurance cover COVID-19? ‚ñº

Many policies now include COVID-19 coverage for medical treatment and trip cancellation if you test positive before departure. However, coverage for cancellation due to travel restrictions or fear of travel is typically excluded. Always read the policy wording carefully and look for specific COVID-19 coverage statements.

When should I buy travel insurance? ‚ñº

Purchase insurance as soon as you make your first trip payment (deposit). This ensures coverage for pre-departure cancellations and may provide coverage for pre-existing conditions if purchased within the specified timeframe (usually 10-21 days of initial payment). Early purchase also protects against unforeseen events that could force cancellation before your trip.

What's not covered by travel insurance? ‚ñº

Common exclusions include: injuries from extreme sports without a rider, losses due to intoxication or illegal activities, pre-existing conditions not declared or purchased within timeframe, cancellations due to "change of mind" or fear of travel, epidemics/pandemics (except specific COVID-19 coverage), war or civil unrest (unless covered by specific clauses), and losses from unreported theft.

How do I make a claim? ‚ñº

1. Contact the 24/7 assistance number immediately for medical emergencies. 2. Keep all documentation: police reports for theft, medical reports and receipts, airline delay/cancellation confirmations, proof of ownership for lost items. 3. Submit claim forms with supporting documents within the required timeframe (typically 90 days). 4. For medical claims, pay upfront if possible and get itemized receipts, though some insurers work directly with hospitals.

Is credit card travel insurance sufficient? ‚ñº

Credit card insurance often has significant limitations: lower coverage limits, exclusion of pre-existing conditions, requirement to charge entire trip to that card, age restrictions, and limited medical coverage. It's suitable for minor trip delays/cancellations but often inadequate for serious medical emergencies. Review your card's benefits guide carefully and consider supplemental coverage for international travel.
