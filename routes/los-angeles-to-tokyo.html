Airlines Flying from Los Angeles to Tokyo

Several major airlines operate flights between Los Angeles and Tokyo, offering various service levels and pricing:

Japan Airlines All Nippon Airways United Airlines American Airlines Delta Air Lines Singapore Airlines Korean Air Asiana Airlines China Airlines Air Canada

Japan Airlines (JAL) and All Nippon Airways (ANA) are the premier choices for this route, offering exceptional Japanese hospitality, modern aircraft, and excellent in-flight service. Both airlines operate multiple daily direct flights from LAX to Tokyo's Narita (NRT) and Haneda (HND) airports. Their service quality, punctuality, and attention to detail make them favorites among frequent travelers to Japan.

United, American, and Delta also provide daily direct service, often at competitive prices. These carriers offer good connectivity to other U.S. cities through their LAX hub and frequent flyer program benefits for loyal customers. While service may not match the Japanese carriers' renowned hospitality, they provide reliable transportation at often lower prices.

Typical Flight Prices from Los Angeles to Tokyo

Los Angeles to Tokyo flight prices vary based on seasonality, booking timing, and cabin class. Understanding these price ranges helps set realistic expectations:

Economy Class: Round-trip economy tickets typically range from $600-$1,200. The lowest fares, around $550-$700, appear during winter months (January-February, excluding New Year) and early fall (September-October). Cherry blossom season (late March-April) and summer vacation period (July-August) see prices spike to $1,000-$1,400 due to high demand from both leisure and business travelers.

Premium Economy: Premium economy seats offering significantly more legroom, better meals, and enhanced service cost $1,200-$2,000 round-trip. For the 11+ hour flight duration, many travelers find this upgrade worthwhile, especially on Japanese carriers known for excellent premium economy products. The extra comfort makes a noticeable difference on such a long flight.

Business Class: Business class fares range from $3,000-$7,000 for round trips. Japanese carriers' business class products are world-renowned, featuring lie-flat seats, exceptional dining, and impeccable service. While expensive, the experience significantly improves long-haul travel comfort. Watch for promotional fares that occasionally drop to $2,500-$3,000, offering excellent value for premium travel.

Best Time to Book Los Angeles to Tokyo Flights

For the Los Angeles to Tokyo route, booking 8-12 weeks in advance typically provides the best economy class pricing. This Pacific route sees strong consistent demand, so waiting too long often means higher prices as cheaper fare buckets fill up. However, airlines sometimes release surprise sales even for near-term travel, especially during off-peak seasons.

Monitor prices starting 3-4 months before your intended travel dates. Transpacific fares fluctuate significantly based on seasonal demand, currency exchange rates, and competitive pressure among airlines. Price tracking tools help you understand the normal fare range for your dates and identify when a good deal appears.

Consider booking positioning if you're flexible. Sometimes flying out a day or two earlier or later can save $200-$400 on the ticket price. Mid-week departures (Tuesday-Thursday) generally cost less than weekend flights, as both business and leisure travelers prefer weekend travel times.

Best Months to Fly to Tokyo

Tokyo offers different experiences throughout the year, with seasonal variations affecting both prices and travel conditions:

Winter (December-February): Winter brings cold but generally clear weather to Tokyo. December sees high prices around the holidays, but January and February offer some of the year's cheapest flights and lowest tourist crowds. Winter illuminations, hot springs, and winter sports in nearby mountains make this season appealing despite cold temperatures. Late February shows increasing prices as spring approaches.

Spring (March-May): Cherry blossom season (late March to early April) is Tokyo's most popular and expensive time to visit. Book extremely early if traveling during sakura season, as flights sell out quickly at premium prices. Early March and May offer better value with pleasant weather and fewer crowds than peak cherry blossom weeks. Spring weather is generally mild and comfortable for sightseeing.

Summer (June-August): June's rainy season brings humidity and frequent showers but lower prices and fewer tourists. July and August are hot and humid with high prices due to summer vacation travel. While not the most comfortable weather for extensive outdoor activities, summer brings festivals, fireworks, and vibrant city energy. Air conditioning is ubiquitous, making indoor attractions comfortable.

Fall (September-November): Fall is arguably Tokyo's best season for visitors, with comfortable temperatures, beautiful autumn foliage, and relatively reasonable flight prices. September offers good value as summer ends. October and November bring stunning fall colors and ideal weather, with November being particularly popular for viewing autumn leaves. Book early for November travel, as it's increasingly recognized as an optimal time to visit.

Los Angeles and Tokyo Airport Information

Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) serves as the primary departure point for Tokyo flights. Located near the Pacific coast in southwestern Los Angeles, LAX is one of the world's busiest airports with extensive ground transportation options including FlyAway buses, Metro rail connections, taxis, and ride-sharing services. International flights depart from the Tom Bradley International Terminal, which offers numerous dining and shopping options.

Tokyo has two major airports serving international flights. Narita International Airport (NRT), located about 60 kilometers east of central Tokyo, handles most international long-haul flights including many Los Angeles routes. The Narita Express train provides comfortable 60-minute connections to central Tokyo, with buses and limousine services also available. While farther from the city, Narita offers a full-service international airport experience.

Haneda Airport (HND), officially Tokyo International Airport, sits much closer to central Tokyo (just 30 minutes by train or monorail). Increasingly, airlines are shifting international flights to Haneda due to its convenient location. If given the choice, Haneda typically provides easier access to Tokyo hotels and attractions. Check which Tokyo airport your flight uses, as this significantly impacts your ground transportation time and costs.

Tips for Finding Cheap Los Angeles to Tokyo Flights

Compare both Narita and Haneda arrival options when searching. While Haneda's proximity to Tokyo is convenient, Narita flights sometimes cost less. Calculate your total door-to-door cost including ground transportation when comparing airports. The time and money saved arriving at Haneda might justify a slightly higher airfare.

Consider one-stop flights via Asian hubs if your schedule allows flexibility. Connections through Seoul, Taipei, or Vancouver can cost $200-$400 less than direct flights. If you're not in a rush and the layover is reasonable (2-4 hours), the savings might be worthwhile. Some travelers even plan extended layovers to explore the connecting city.

Watch for airline alliance sales and promotional fares. The Los Angeles to Tokyo route frequently features in special offers, particularly during shoulder seasons. Subscribe to airline newsletters and follow deal alert accounts to catch these limited-time promotions that can dramatically reduce ticket prices.

Be flexible with your return date if possible. Sometimes extending or shortening your trip by a day or two unlocks significantly better fares. Use flexible date search tools to compare prices across different return dates, as mid-week returns typically cost less than Sunday flights when business travelers return home.
